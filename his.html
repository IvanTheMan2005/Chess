6th Century: Origins of Chess - The game of chess is believed to have originated in northern India during the 6th century. It was known as chaturanga and featured different military units represented by pieces on a board.

1851

First International Chess Tournament

The first international chess tournament was held in London in 1851. The tournament attracted top players from various countries, and Adolf Anderssen emerged as the winner, solidifying his reputation as one of the leading chess players of the time.

New

1866

Steinitz Becomes World Chess Champion

Wilhelm Steinitz of Austria-Hungary became the first officially recognized World Chess Champion in 1886. He held the title until 1894, introducing strategic concepts and the idea of positional play, which revolutionized chess strategy.

1948

FIDE World Chess Championship Established

The FIDE World Chess Championship, governed by the International Chess Federation (FIDE), was established in 1948. The first FIDE World Chess Championship match was held in 1948, won by Mikhail Botvinnik, who went on to dominate chess for several years.

1972

Fischer vs. Spassky Match

The 1972 World Chess Championship match between Bobby Fischer of the United States and Boris Spassky of the Soviet Union became one of the most iconic moments in chess history. Fischer's victory marked a turning point in chess, popularizing the game worldwide and sparking a surge of interest in the United States.
