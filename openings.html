Queens Gambit the classic beauty 12 Feb 2022

The Queen's Gambit is a chess opening that starts with the moves 1. d4 d5 2. c4. It is a popular and aggressive opening that is named after the strategy of sacrificing a pawn in the center of the board (the Queen's Gambit) to gain control and develop pieces quickly.

Ivan Castaneda

The Caro-Kann Defence 15 Jan 2002

The Caro-Kann is known as a solid and reliable defense against 1.e4, where Black aims to control the center with their pawn on d5. The opening is named after the English player Horatio Caro and the Austrian player Marcus Kann

Jayden Garvey

The Kings Gambit 20 Aug 2005

With the King's Gambit, White sacrifices a pawn on f4 to gain control of the center and initiate an attacking position. The opening aims to put pressure on Black's pawn structure and open up lines for rapid development and piece activity.

Adrian Nolte Sala

DEFENCE

The King's Indian Defence (Comming soon...) 23 Sep 2023

In the King's Indian Defense, Black allows White to establish a strong presence in the center with pawns on d4 and e4. Instead, Black focuses on a flexible pawn structure and plans to counter-attack against White's center in the later stages of the game.

Daniel Amirbekyan

English Opening (Comming soon...) 1 Jan 1992

The English Opening is a chess opening that is characterized by the initial move 1.c4. It is named after the English players who popularized it in the 19th century. The English Opening aims to control the center and create a flexible pawn structure for White.

Brandon Shi

ATTACK

Benko Gambit (Comming soon...) 13 Oct 2003

The Benko Gambit is a pawn sacrifice by Black in order to generate counterplay and create imbalances in the position. By playing 3...b5, Black offers the b5 pawn to White, aiming to open up the queenside and create pressure against White's position.

Xavier Livesay
